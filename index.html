Scoring Philosophy

XP Score — Raw Impact Index

The XP Score is a high‑resolution performance metric that captures a player’s impact on the field. It’s intentionally unfiltered — built from core stats like BA, OBP, SLG, BB%, and K% — and scaled so explosive players stand out.

XP rewards:

  • High batting average
  • Strong on‑base skill
  • Slugging power
  • Walk discipline
  • Low strikeout rates

XP is designed to pop. When a hitter like Ben Rice posts a 1246 XP, it tells you he’s doing something special — even if his traditional stats don’t immediately show it. XP reveals outliers, breakout candidates, and fantasy sleepers.

Overall Score — 5‑Metric Skill Model (0–10)

The Overall Score is a balanced, normalized rating built from five skill components:

  • BA — Contact Ability
  • OBP — Discipline & On‑Base Skill
  • SLG — Power
  • K% — Bat‑to‑Ball Skill
  • BB% — Walk Discipline

Each metric is scaled from 0–10 and weighted to produce a final Overall Score. This model reflects true skill and consistency — the kind of rating scouts and analysts rely on.

Why Both Scores Matter

XP shows impact. Overall Score shows skill. Together, they tell the full story of a player’s performance.
